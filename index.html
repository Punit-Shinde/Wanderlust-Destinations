Haridwar

India

Rishikesh

India

Haridwar, literally meaning "the abode of Lord Vishnu", is a place where millions of people flock every year to take part in an annual pilgrimage. It is also the gateway to the Char Dham, a set of four pilgrimage sites in India. One of the main highlights of Haridwar is the Kumbh Mela, a massive religious gathering held every twelve years. The city is also known for the magnificent Ganga Aarti that takes place at the Har-Ki-Pauri Ghat. This event, with flaming torches moved in harmonious synchronisation by the Pandits, is a truly unique and lively sight.

Rishikesh, on the other hand, is known as the "World Capital of Yoga". It is situated at a higher altitude and is located by the roaring Ganga. The city is quite rustic and offers you the authentic experience of Uttarakhand. It is also renowned for its natural beauty, making it a perfect weekend getaway.
